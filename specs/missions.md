# Ghost Driver — Missions

## Philosophy
Every mission is a story told through driving. The briefing sets the scene, the chase is the conflict, the ditch is the resolution. No cutscene should do what a well-designed chase can do on its own.

---

## Mission Structure Overview
The campaign is divided into three Acts, each escalating in complexity, cop aggression, and narrative stakes. The demo contains Acts 1 and the first mission of Act 2 — enough to hook the player without giving away the full story.

| Act | Name | Missions | Focus |
|-----|------|----------|-------|
| 1 | The Ghost Emerges | 5 missions | Learn the city, pure escape, establish character |
| 2 | The Price of Notoriety | 8 missions | Mid-chase objectives, vehicle management, faction tension |
| 3 | No Way Out | 7 missions | Multi-step objectives, full pursuit pressure, campaign climax |

**Total: 20 missions** — substantial for an indie campaign, completable in 6-10 hours depending on skill level.

---

## Mission Anatomy
Every mission follows the same structural skeleton, with complexity layered on top:

1. **Briefing** — noir-style text card with illustrated backdrop. Sets the job, the client, the stakes. Voice of the Ghost Driver (internal monologue). No lengthy cutscenes.
2. **Garage / Loadout** — select vehicle, equip gadgets, review notoriety status
3. **Drop-in** — mission begins mid-action. Job is already done. Cops are already coming.
4. **Chase Phase** — active pursuit. Survive, evade, think.
5. **Objective Phase** (mid/late missions) — cooldown window opens. Complete task before timer expires or cops re-acquire.
6. **Ditch Phase** — fully lose pursuit. Cooldown timer runs to zero.
7. **Mission Complete** — "GHOST" flash. Cash reward. Notoriety update. Unlock check.

---

## Job Types
The type of job determines the mission's opening scenario and flavor. The player is always the getaway driver — they never see the job itself, only its aftermath.

- **Bank Job** — escape from a downtown financial district, heavy cop presence from the start
- **Package Pickup** — retrieve something from a contact mid-chase, requires a cooldown window to make the exchange
- **Package Drop** — deliver something to a location, must ditch cops before arriving (can't lead them to the drop point)
- **Extraction** — pick someone up from a location and deliver them safely, passenger affects vehicle handling slightly
- **Dead Drop** — leave something at a location without being seen, requires full ghost status to complete
- **Decoy Run** — you're the distraction while someone else does the real job, survive as long as possible under maximum pressure
- **Repo** — repossess a specific vehicle parked somewhere in the city, switch vehicles mid-mission
- **Ghost Protocol** — late campaign. Multi-stage job combining elements of several types under maximum pursuit.

## Act 1 — The Ghost Emerges
*"Nobody knows your name yet. That's about to change."*

The player is new to the city. Missions are pure escape — no mid-chase objectives. The focus is learning car handling, understanding cop behavior, and discovering the ditch mechanic. The city reveals itself gradually.

### Mission 1 — First Run
**Job type:** Bank Job
**Vehicle required:** Any (player starts with one clean sedan)
**Gadgets:** Oil Slick only (tutorial gadget)
**Pursuit level cap:** 2
**Objective:** Escape the financial district and ditch pursuit
**Notes:** Tutorial mission. On-screen prompts guide the player through controls, handbrake, minimap, and the cooldown mechanic. Short and forgiving. Ends with the first "GHOST" flash — player feels the fantasy immediately.
**Reward:** $800, Nitro Boost unlocked

### Mission 2 — Side Streets
**Job type:** Package Drop
**Vehicle required:** Sedan
**Gadgets:** Oil Slick, Nitro Boost
**Pursuit level cap:** 2
**Objective:** Ditch pursuit completely before reaching the drop point — can't lead cops to the location
**Notes:** Introduces the concept that the objective can't be completed under active pursuit. Player must find their first real cooldown window. Map opens up slightly — back alleys introduced.
**Reward:** $1,200

### Mission 3 — The Long Way Round
**Job type:** Extraction
**Vehicle required:** Any
**Gadgets:** Player choice (from unlocked)
**Pursuit level cap:** 3
**Objective:** Pick up a contact from across the city, deliver them to a safehouse
**Notes:** First mission with a passenger. Slight handling change with passenger weight. Longer route forces exploration of more of the city. Introduces parking garages as ditch locations.
**Reward:** $1,500, Smoke Screen unlocked

### Mission 4 — Hot Wheels
**Job type:** Repo
**Vehicle required:** Sedan (start), Sports Car (acquired mid-mission)
**Gadgets:** Player choice
**Pursuit level cap:** 3
**Objective:** Reach a parked sports car across the city, switch into it, escape in the new vehicle
**Notes:** First mid-mission vehicle swap. Introduces the idea that different vehicles handle differently mid-chase. The sports car is faster but twitchier — player feels the difference immediately. New vehicle enters garage after mission.
**Reward:** $2,000, Sports Car added to garage

### Mission 5 — Ghost Story
**Job type:** Decoy Run
**Vehicle required:** Sports Car
**Gadgets:** Player choice
**Pursuit level cap:** 4
**Objective:** Survive under maximum pursuit pressure for a set duration while a partner completes the real job elsewhere
**Notes:** First time pursuit hits level 4. Helicopter introduced. Player learns helicopter counter-play. No ditch required — survive the timer instead. Act 1 closes with the Ghost Driver's reputation beginning to spread. Briefing afterward hints at a larger faction taking notice.
**Reward:** $2,500, Repair Kit unlocked, Act 2 unlocked

## Act 2 — The Price of Notoriety
*"They know your name now. That's the problem."*

The Ghost Driver is now a known commodity in the criminal underworld. Better jobs, better pay — but the cops know something is coordinating these runs. Mid-chase objectives become standard. Vehicle management matters. A criminal faction called The Syndicate begins using the Ghost Driver for increasingly dangerous work, and a detective begins building a case.

### Mission 6 — The Exchange
**Job type:** Package Pickup
**Vehicle required:** Sedan (clean — low notoriety required for contact to approach)
**Gadgets:** Player choice
**Pursuit level cap:** 3
**Objective:** Ditch pursuit temporarily, make a mid-chase exchange with a contact on a specific street corner, then fully ditch
**Notes:** First true mid-chase objective. Player must achieve cooldown state, navigate to the contact, complete the exchange, then re-ditch if pursuit resumes. Introduces the pressure of the objective window.
**Reward:** $3,000

### Mission 7 — Ironclad
**Job type:** Bank Job
**Vehicle required:** Armored Truck
**Gadgets:** Player choice
**Pursuit level cap:** 3
**Objective:** Escape in the armored truck — slow but nearly impervious to ramming
**Notes:** First armored truck mission. Player feels the weight difference immediately. Can ram through roadblocks that would stop other vehicles. Cops can't easily disable it but can surround and slow it. New tactical puzzle.
**Reward:** $3,500, Armored Truck added to garage

### Mission 8 — Blackout ⚑ UNDERCOVER
**Job type:** Dead Drop
**Vehicle required:** Work van (cover vehicle), Any (swap car)
**Gadgets:** Player choice (unavailable during undercover phase)
**Pursuit level cap:** 3
**Undercover objective:** Drive a utility van through a cop-heavy district at night, reach the dead drop location and make the drop without being noticed
**Blown cover:** Sprint to a hidden sedan two blocks from the drop point
**Notes:** First undercover mission. Night setting makes Kill Lights meaningful during the escape phase. Stealth path rewards patience — complete the drop cleanly and walk away with zero pursuit. Guns blazing path is chaotic but viable. Introduces the mechanic gently with a forgiving pursuit cap.
**Reward:** $3,000 base, +$1,500 bonus for clean undercover completion. Scrambler unlocked.

### Mission 9 — Double Cross
**Job type:** Package Drop
**Vehicle required:** Sports Car
**Gadgets:** Player choice
**Pursuit level cap:** 4
**Objective:** Deliver the package — mid-mission the contact tips off the cops to the drop location, forcing a route change
**Notes:** First mission with a dynamic objective change mid-chase. Player must adapt on the fly. Introduces the narrative thread that not everyone in the underworld can be trusted. Spike strips appear for the first time.
**Reward:** $4,000, Spike Strips unlocked

### Mission 10 — Borrowed Time
**Job type:** Extraction
**Vehicle required:** SUV
**Gadgets:** Player choice
**Pursuit level cap:** 4
**Objective:** Extract a high-value passenger, deliver them across the city — passenger is injured and vehicle cannot exceed a speed threshold or take heavy damage
**Notes:** Speed limiter mechanic for this mission only. Forces patient, tactical driving over raw speed. Most tense mission so far — high pursuit with a fragile objective.
**Reward:** $4,500, SUV added to garage

### Mission 11 — The Frame
**Job type:** Decoy Run
**Vehicle required:** Any (high notoriety vehicle recommended — you're meant to be seen)
**Gadgets:** Player choice
**Pursuit level cap:** 5
**Objective:** Draw maximum police attention across the city while The Syndicate moves assets
**Notes:** First pursuit level 5 mission. Helicopter and elite units. Player is intentionally the distraction — notoriety on the used vehicle will spike. Tests mastery of all evasion tools learned so far.
**Reward:** $5,000, Chassis Cloak unlocked

### Mission 12 — Blind Spot ⚑ UNDERCOVER
**Job type:** Ghost Protocol (early version)
**Vehicle required:** Police cruiser (cover vehicle), Sports Car (swap car — clean)
**Gadgets:** Player choice (unavailable during undercover phase)
**Pursuit level cap:** 4
**Undercover objective:** Impersonate a patrol officer — drive a police cruiser through a checkpoint and deliver a package to a contact on the other side without raising suspicion
**Blown cover:** Immediately hunted by the entire force — cop impersonation escalates pursuit faster than any other mission. Sprint to a hidden sports car on the far side of the checkpoint.
**Notes:** Most dramatic undercover mission — you're driving their own vehicle against them. Clean completion is deeply satisfying. Blown cover is the most intense chase in Act 2. The irony of being chased by police in a police car is intentional flavor.
**Reward:** $5,500 base, +$2,000 bonus for clean completion. Decoy Beacon unlocked.

### Mission 13 — The Detective
**Job type:** Package Drop
**Vehicle required:** Any
**Gadgets:** Player choice
**Pursuit level cap:** 5
**Objective:** Standard drop — but a named detective character is personally coordinating pursuit for the first time
**Notes:** Narrative turning point. The detective is smarter than standard AI — anticipates routes more accurately, calls in resources faster. First hint of a personal antagonist. Act 2 closes with The Syndicate demanding one final impossible job. The Ghost Driver considers walking away.
**Reward:** $6,000, EMP Blast unlocked, Act 3 unlocked

## Act 3 — No Way Out
*"You should have disappeared when you had the chance."*

The Ghost Driver is now the most wanted getaway driver in the city. The detective has built a task force specifically targeting them. The Syndicate is using the Ghost Driver's reputation as leverage. Every mission is high stakes — full pursuit tools deployed from the start, multi-step objectives, and the campaign's narrative driving toward a final confrontation with the detective.

### Mission 14 — Task Force
**Job type:** Bank Job
**Vehicle required:** Any (high slot count recommended)
**Gadgets:** Full loadout essential
**Pursuit level cap:** 5
**Objective:** Escape the most coordinated pursuit response yet — the task force has pre-positioned units across the city
**Notes:** Opens Act 3 with maximum pressure. Pre-positioned roadblocks and interceptors require route planning before the mission starts. Minimap reading becomes critical.
**Reward:** $7,000, Grappling Hook unlocked

### Mission 15 — Switchback
**Job type:** Repo
**Vehicle required:** Sedan (start), target vehicle (acquired mid-mission)
**Gadgets:** Player choice
**Pursuit level cap:** 5
**Objective:** Steal a specific vehicle from a police impound lot — maximum security, maximum pursuit on exit
**Notes:** Stealing from the police directly. Pursuit begins at level 4 the moment the player enters the impound. New vehicle is an exotic — fastest in the game. High narrative stakes.
**Reward:** $8,000, Exotic added to garage

### Mission 16 — Collateral ⚑ UNDERCOVER
**Job type:** Dead Drop
**Vehicle required:** Taxi (cover vehicle), Sedan (swap car — low notoriety)
**Gadgets:** Player choice (unavailable during undercover phase)
**Pursuit level cap:** 4
**Undercover objective:** Operate a taxi in a densely civilian area, pick up a specific fare who is actually a contact, complete the exchange during the ride without other passengers noticing
**Blown cover:** The contact panics and runs — cops are called. Swap car is parked at a nearby cab rank.
**Notes:** Most subtle undercover mission — the objective happens inside the vehicle during a fake fare. Civilian density still applies during chase phase. Moral weight of the civilian setting comes through in both paths. Clean completion requires the most patience of any undercover mission.
**Reward:** $7,500 base, +$2,500 bonus for clean completion.

### Mission 17 — The Syndicate's Play
**Job type:** Ghost Protocol
**Vehicle required:** Player choice (multiple vehicles available at garage between stages)
**Gadgets:** Full loadout
**Pursuit level cap:** 5
**Objective:** Three-stage mission — bank job escape, mid-city exchange, final delivery to docks — each stage escalates pursuit
**Notes:** Longest mission in the game. Garage visit between stages 1 and 2 allows vehicle and gadget swap. Tests mastery of every mechanic. Narrative reveal — The Syndicate's true plan comes to light. Ghost Driver realizes they've been used.
**Reward:** $10,000

### Mission 18 — Burn It Down
**Job type:** Decoy Run
**Vehicle required:** High notoriety vehicle (intentional — this vehicle will be burned after)
**Gadgets:** Player choice
**Pursuit level cap:** 5
**Objective:** Maximum distraction run — survive the full timer under task force pursuit
**Notes:** Narrative mission — Ghost Driver is buying time for something. The vehicle used is burned at mission end as part of the story. Emotional weight to losing a vehicle the player has used throughout the campaign.
**Reward:** $8,000 + vehicle burn bonus

### Mission 19 — Ghost Protocol
**Job type:** Ghost Protocol (full)
**Vehicle required:** Best available
**Gadgets:** Full loadout, all upgrades recommended
**Pursuit level cap:** 5 (task force + detective personally in pursuit)
**Objective:** Final job — four stages across the entire city map, the detective is personally coordinating pursuit in real time
**Notes:** The penultimate mission. Detective AI is the hardest pursuit element in the game — learns from the player's patterns across the mission. Routes the player has used repeatedly get cut off. Requires genuine adaptability.
**Reward:** $12,000

### Mission 20 — Vanish
**Job type:** Ghost Protocol (epilogue)
**Vehicle required:** Any — player chooses their farewell vehicle
**Gadgets:** Player choice
**Pursuit level cap:** 5
**Objective:** One final run. Escape the city entirely. Reach the highway at the map's edge and disappear.
**Notes:** The campaign's final statement. No mid-chase objectives — pure escape, pure driving, pure Ghost Driver. The detective makes one last attempt. The ditch mechanic pays off one final time. Ends on the "GHOST" flash — then silence. Epilogue text card. Nobody knows where the Ghost Driver went. Nobody ever will.
**Reward:** Campaign complete. Credits. New Game+ unlocked.

## New Game+
After completing the campaign, New Game+ becomes available:
- All missions replayable with full gadget and vehicle roster
- Optional challenge modifiers per mission (no gadgets, time limits, pursuit starts at level 3 minimum)
- Hidden ghost routes — secret paths through the city that bypass standard cop positions, discoverable only through exploration
- Detective appears in earlier missions in NG+ — he's been hunting longer than you knew

---

## Mission Rewards Summary
- **Cash** — primary currency for vehicles, gadgets, upgrades, visual mods
- **Gadget unlocks** — tied to specific missions, can't be bought early
- **Vehicle unlocks** — some earned through missions, others purchased
- **Narrative progression** — briefings, character reveals, world building
- **Star ratings** — optional. Each mission rated 1-3 stars based on performance (time, pursuit level reached, civilian collisions, gadgets used). Completionists have a goal beyond just finishing.
