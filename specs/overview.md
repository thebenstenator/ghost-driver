# Ghost Driver — Project Overview

## Tagline
*"They never see you coming. They never see you go."*

## Concept
Ghost Driver is a top-down 2D arcade-noir chase game where you play as a legendary getaway driver in a retro-futuristic 1920s-inspired city. Every mission drops you in at the end of a job — the heist is done, the clock is ticking, and the cops are already on their way. Your only job is to vanish.

## Setting
A retro-futuristic noir city — think 1920s Art Deco architecture, rain-slicked streets, and jazz-era culture, but with advanced technology woven in. Holographic wanted posters. Turbocharged rumble-seat cars. Neon signage over speakeasy storefronts. The world feels timeless and cinematic — dark, stylish, and dangerous.

## The Character
The Ghost Driver is a legend in the criminal underworld. Nobody knows who they are. Criminals hire them specifically. Cops fear the name. The player *is* the Ghost Driver — identity unknown, reputation everything.

## Core Loop
1. Accept a job (bank robbery, package pickup, extraction, etc.)
2. Mission begins *after* the job — cops already alerted, pursuit incoming
3. Navigate the city, complete any mid-mission objectives during cooldown windows
4. Fully ditch the cops to complete the mission (pursuit cooldown expires)
5. Earn money, build notoriety, unlock new vehicles and jobs

## The Ditch Mechanic
The standout mechanic of Ghost Driver. Escaping pursuit isn't just about outrunning cops — it requires genuine skill:
- Hide in parking garages
- Kill your lights on dark streets
- Use decoy routes to mislead pursuit
- Break line of sight long enough for the pursuit cooldown to expire
- Cops give up only when they've fully lost you — not just when you're far away

## Mission Structure
**Early missions** — Pure escape. Learn the city, learn the controls, learn how cops behave.

**Mid missions** — Escape *while* doing something. Drop a package, pick someone up, avoid specific roads. Tasks can only be completed during a cooldown window (temporary ditch), not during active pursuit.

**Late missions** — Multi-step objectives under full pursuit. Roadblocks, helicopter surveillance, spike strips. Requires mastery of the ditch mechanic and smart vehicle selection.

## Notoriety System
- Notoriety is earned per vehicle, not per player
- Higher notoriety = faster police response, more aggressive AI, harder jobs
- Players must manage a garage of vehicles — switching cars to control risk
- Different jobs require different vehicles (sports cars, armored trucks, nondescript sedans)
- A high-notoriety vehicle becomes a liability — sometimes you burn it

## Vehicles
A varied roster tied to job requirements and playstyle:
- **Sports cars** — fast, fragile, high notoriety risk
- **Armored trucks** — slow, can ram through blockades, specific job requirements
- **Nondescript sedans** — low notoriety, blend in, useful for early cooldown phases
- **Specialty vehicles** — unlocked through campaign progression

Each vehicle has a front 3/4 and rear 3/4 detailed illustration viewable in the garage screen, giving players a cinematic look at their fleet beyond the top-down gameplay sprite.

## Cop AI Behavior
- Pursuit units chase but also attempt to box in and intercept
- Longer chases trigger reinforcements (more units, roadblocks, eventually helicopters)
- Cops communicate — flanking routes get covered over time
- Pursuit difficulty scales with notoriety level of the active vehicle

## Visual Style
**Gameplay** — Clean, readable top-down 2D. Minimal polygon count. Dark palette with neon accent colors. Prioritizes clarity over detail.

**Garage / Menus** — Higher detail 2D illustrations. Front 3/4 and rear 3/4 car views. Rain reflections, neon lighting, cinematic framing. This is where the art breathes.

**Color Palette** — Deep blacks, midnight blues, amber streetlights, neon reds and greens. Rain is always present or implied.

## Monetization (Locked)
- **Free demo** — 3-4 missions, 2-3 vehicles, enough to experience the core loop
- **One-time purchase** unlocks the full campaign and everything in it — no IAP, no cosmetic treadmill, no live service
- Launch on itch.io first, Steam once polished
- Philosophy: pay once, get everything. No tricks.
- Cosmetics only reconsidered if multiplayer is ever built, and even then likely included in base price

## Future Scope (Post-MVP)
- Cop mode — play as pursuit units trying to catch the Ghost Driver
- Multiplayer — one Ghost Driver vs several cop players
- Additional cities / map expansions
- Ghost Driver: Origins — prequel campaign

## MVP Goal
A single city map, a handful of missions across all three difficulty tiers, 3-5 vehicles, functional notoriety system, and a ditch mechanic that feels genuinely satisfying. If escaping feels clever and earned, the MVP is a success.
